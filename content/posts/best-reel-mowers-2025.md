Gas mowers roar. Electric mowers whirr. But a reel mower — a reel mower whispers. The soft "snikt-snikt-snikt" of spinning blades against a stationary bed knife is the sound of a Sunday morning that doesn't wake the neighbors.

Reel mowers cut grass by scissoring each blade between a spinning reel and a fixed bed knife — a fundamentally cleaner cut than the tearing action of a rotary mower. The result? Healthier grass that's less susceptible to disease and brown tips. We tested 6 of the best reel mowers for lawns from 500 to 5,000 square feet.

## What to Look For

### Cutting Width

Reel mowers come in 14-inch to 20-inch widths. A 14-inch mower requires more passes — add ~5 extra minutes per 1,000 sq ft compared to a 20-inch model. For lawns under 1,000 sq ft, 14-inch is fine. For 2,000–5,000 sq ft, get 18–20 inches.

### Blade Quality

The difference between a $80 and a $200 reel mower is almost entirely in the blades. High-carbon steel blades stay sharp longer and handle thicker grass. Heat-treated alloy blades are the premium option — they hold an edge for an entire season. Avoid "tempered steel" without a specific alloy designation — it dulls quickly.

### Height Adjustment

Most reel mowers adjust between 0.5 and 2.75 inches. Easy adjustment matters — if changing the height requires tools and 5 minutes, you'll never do it. The best mowers have a single-lever system that adjusts all four wheels simultaneously.

### Weight

Reel mowers weigh 15–35 lbs. Lighter mowers are easier to push but may bounce over uneven ground. Heavier mowers track more consistently through thick grass. For flat, small lawns, lighter is better. For bumpy terrain or thick grass, some heft helps.

## FAQ

### Will a reel mower work on my lawn?

Reel mowers work best on cool-season grasses (Kentucky bluegrass, fescue, rye) and lawns under 5,000 sq ft. They struggle with tall, thick southern grasses like Bermuda and St. Augustine — the tough stems get pushed over instead of cut. They also struggle with lawns that go more than a week between mowings. Reel mowers reward consistent, frequent mowing.

### Do I really never need to sharpen the blades?

Fiskars' StaySharp system genuinely doesn't need sharpening under normal use. All other reel mowers need annual sharpening — either DIY with a lapping compound kit ($15) or professional sharpening ($25–40). Budgeting one sharpening per year is realistic for most reel mowers.

### Is a reel mower actually better for the grass?

Yes. Rotary mowers tear grass with a horizontal blade. Reel mowers cut it with a scissor action. The clean cut heals faster, loses less moisture, and is less susceptible to fungus. Golf courses use reel mowers for a reason — they produce the healthiest turf. The trade-off is that reel mowers require more frequent mowing to maintain that quality.
