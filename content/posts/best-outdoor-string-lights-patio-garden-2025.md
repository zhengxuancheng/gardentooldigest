## FAQ

### How do I hang string lights without drilling?

For soft ground, use 24-inch metal stakes with a hook on top. For decks and patios, use adhesive clips rated for outdoor use. For wood surfaces, use eye hooks (one small pilot hole). Tension wire between two anchor points is the best long-term solution — string the wire first, then hang your lights on it with zip ties or hooks.

### How do I calculate how many lights I need?

Measure the total length of your run and divide by the bulb spacing. For dining areas, bulbs every 12 inches provides enough light for eating and games. For ambient mood lighting, bulbs every 18–24 inches works. General rule: a standard 12x12 foot patio needs about 40 feet of string lights with bulbs spaced 12 inches apart.

### Can outdoor string lights stay up in winter?

It depends on the bulb and wire rating. All six lights we tested are rated for year-round outdoor use, but ice and snow can weigh down the string and damage connectors. If you live in a heavy snow area, take down the lights before winter or use a tension wire to support the weight. Plastic bulbs handle cold better than glass — glass can crack in freeze-thaw cycles if moisture gets inside.

### What's the difference between S14, G40, and Edison bulbs?

S14 bulbs are torpedo-shaped (1.5 inches long, 0.75 inches wide) — the classic restaurant-style bulbs. G40 bulbs are round globes (1.5 inches diameter) with a softer, more diffused glow. Edison bulbs are the large, cage-style bulbs with visible filaments — they put out the most distinctive warm glow but are also the most fragile. G40 and Edison are available in shatterproof plastic; S14 is traditionally glass.
